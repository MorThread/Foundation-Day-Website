Cricket Field: A study haven for Emilians

As the world around us begins to move at a rapid pace as a result of technological development, we are regrettably surrounded by tons of distractions and noises that should be avoided for a while. We humans, like other animals, want a place where we can find peace of mind. A place where we may think more critically about our own problems, grow, and have plenty of time to read and gather insight. Such a location is difficult to come by these days when everything is being revolutionized by technology and infrastructure. Fortunately for us Emilians, we have that kind of refuge on campus, our very own cricket field in the heart of campus.

There are several benches scattered across the school cricket field that are positioned between big trees that provide shade from the sweltering sun, fresh breezes, and umbrellas for light rain. Along with the sounds of nature—the wind, birds, insects, and greenery—the trees also provide seclusion and a sense that you are in your own place or with your companions. All of this, together with its ideal spot away from any industrial sounds outside, completely envelops the area's beauty and embracing environment. A location for enjoyment, recollections, and above all, the tranquility it offers those who seek it for focus and study. Most students use this space to get ideas for work and to review for forthcoming courses and exams, while others use it to form friendships and relationships with friends and classmates. Such a place for learning and engagement.

Touching on, it is undeniable that when you learn how to care for nature, nature nurtures you back. That is the type of mutual partnership that everyone desires.
